A City of Heritage and Harmony

In the heart of Muscat, where the sea kisses the stone and the mountains stand guard, rises a city that whispers history at every turn. Here, turquoise minarets and ornate domes reflect centuries of devotion, craftsmanship, and cultural pride.


Muscat is not just the capital of Oman — it is its soul. A place where calligraphy dances across mosque walls, and wooden balconies overlook ancient harbors. As daylight fades, the city glows in golden hues, inviting you to walk its peaceful streets and feel its timeless rhythm.


This city embraces its visitors with a rare elegance — offering not only sights to behold, but stories to feel. Every corner tells of a nation rooted in hospitality, faith, and deep respect for tradition. Yet beneath this ancient beauty lies a quiet modernity, welcoming the future with grace.


Whether you're wandering the corridors of old Muttrah Souq, hearing the call to prayer echo across the corniche, or simply watching the sun dip behind the Hajar mountains, Muscat promises more than a visit — it offers a memory engraved in the heart.

Do I need a visa to visit Oman?

Yes, but many nationalities can easily apply online for an eVisa through the official Royal Oman Police portal.

What is the best time to visit Oman?

The ideal travel period is between October and April, when the weather is mild, especially for desert and mountain activities.

Is Oman safe for tourists?

Oman is considered one of the safest countries in the world, with a welcoming and respectful culture towards visitors.

What should I wear while visiting Oman?

Modest clothing is appreciated, especially in religious or traditional areas. Lightweight long sleeves and trousers are best for the climate.

Can I drive in Oman with my international license?

Yes, international driving licenses are accepted. Renting a 4x4 is highly recommended if you plan to visit mountainous or desert regions.
